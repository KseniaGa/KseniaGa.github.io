About Me

I am Kseniia Harshina, currently a University Assistant at the University of Klagenfurt, Austria. My research focuses on developing a Virtual-Reality Game for Empathy Enhancement and Perspective-Taking in the Context of Refugee Experiences. I have a diverse background in game studies and engineering, with significant experience in machine learning, swarm intelligence, and game design.

I completed my M.Sc. in Game Studies and Engineering from Alpen-Adria University, Klagenfurt, where my master's thesis explored generating UAV countermeasures using machine learning. I hold a B.Sc. in Software Engineering from the National Technical University of Ukraine 'Igor Sikorsky Kyiv Polytechnic Institute.'

Research

My current research projects include:

  • Developing a Virtual-Reality Game for Empathy Enhancement and Perspective-Taking in the Context of Refugee Experiences (Ada Lovelace research project).
  • Generating UAV swarm countermeasures using Reinforcement Learning (MCUAS research project).
  • Optimizing industrial plants using Swarm Intelligence (ML&Swarms project).
  • Edge-Cloud Infrastructure optimization using Swarm Intelligence (ACES project).

I have also worked on various projects during my master's and bachelor's studies, including a master’s thesis on generating UAV countermeasures using machine learning and a bachelor’s thesis on a project management web application focused on volunteering projects.
